About Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world, standing at 8,849 meters (29,032 feet) above sea level. Located in the Himalayas on the border of Nepal and Tibet, it attracts climbers and adventurers from across the globe.

Quick Facts

  • Height: 8,849 m (29,032 ft)
  • Location: Nepal–Tibet border
  • First Ascent: 1953 by Sir Edmund Hillary & Tenzing Norgay

Planning a Visit

Visitors often explore the Everest Base Camp trek, a challenging yet rewarding journey through the Himalayas. Spring (April–May) and Autumn (September–November) are the best times for trekking.
